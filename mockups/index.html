Direction A · Original
Lepakshi
లేపాక్షి శైలి

Inspired by the Vijayanagara-era frescoes at Lepakshi temple in your own Anantapur district. Terracotta + turmeric + indigo on ivory. Newspaper density preserved with ornamental fresco-style dividers and asymmetric pull-quotes. Feels like a regional cultural institution — modernized but rooted.

Display: NTR / Suranna
Body: Noto Serif Telugu
Vibe: Regional gravitas. Modern but timeless.
Best for: Differentiation from Eenadu while staying serious

Direction B
Editorial Restraint
సంపాదకీయ నైతికత

The Hindu × NYT for Telugu. Ink black on cream paper, one deep-maroon accent, hairline rules, generous white space. Asymmetric layouts, serif numerals, single-column long-form. Massive differentiation from any existing Telugu paper — positions you as the thinking person's regional newspaper.

Display: Suranna + Spectral
Body: Noto Serif Telugu
Vibe: Premium, considered, aspirational.
Best for: Urban diaspora, subscriber paywall

Direction C
Folk Maximalist
జానపద శైలి

Kalamkari + Cheriyal scrolls + Tholu Bommalata (Anantapur leather puppet art). Vermilion + indigo + turmeric on cream. Hand-stamped breaking-news badges, patterned dividers, hard offset shadows on cards. Dense like Eenadu but ornamented with regional craft motifs. Loud, proud, unmistakable.

Display: Ramabhadra + Yatra One
Body: Mandali
Vibe: Cultural pride, folk-art warmth.
Best for: Younger regional readers, emotional pull

How to compare them

Open each mockup full-screen. Look at: (1) how the masthead feels, (2) typography hierarchy, (3) breaking-news treatment, (4) how district cards differentiate from category cards, (5) the editorial pull-quote / opinion treatment, (6) the footer.

Each mockup uses the same content (real Telugu headlines from the categories already in your DB) so the differences you feel are purely design, not content.

Once you pick a direction, I'll port it into the Next.js app — global CSS variables, Tailwind config, and updates to apps/web/src/components/* — replacing the current Eenadu-clone aesthetic.
